# Genomic signatures of adaptation across a landscape of crickets following the introduction of a parasitoid fly

## Abstract
Novel species interactions provide an opportunity to assess the earliest stages of genetic adaptation. We combined population genomics and field selection surveys to explore the genomic and geographic landscape of adaptation in small, fragmented Hawaiian cricket populations, which are parasitised by larvae of a recently-introduced fly that targets singing males. Multiple protective male-silencing cricket morphs have recently spread under this novel selection pressure, despite song’s important role in mate attraction. We find evidence of sharp declines in cricket effective population sizes following the fly’s introduction, with geographical variation in selection influencing the frequency of alleles associated with a recently-emerged male-silencing phenotype, as well in other genes implicated in resisting infestation. Despite repeated bottlenecks, genetic variation is dominated by polymorphic structural variants that appear to be maintained under balancing selection. Our study illustrates pervasive consequences of abrupt changes in selection in this example of rapidly adapting cricket populations, and raises questions regarding its suitability as an example of evolutionary rescue.

## Directories

#### aln_variant_calling

Bash scripts used to clean and align reads to the genome, filter them and call variants, and filter variants.

#### Fig1_map_locations

R scripts and files used to produce Figure 1 of the text, showing a map of populations and barplots of morph frequencies.

#### Fig2_SVs

Bash and R scripts used to perform exploratory analyses to investigate the presence of structural variants, and plot them for Figure 2 of the text.

#### Fig3_pop_structure_dem_inference

Bash, R and Python scripts used to visualise population structure (PCA, phylogenetic trees, Fst) and to infer changes in effective population size based on coalescence and linkage disequilibrium, as well as plot them for Figure 3 of the text.

#### Fig4_fly_gwas_selection_de

Bash and R scripts to test geographic and temporal variation in selection (fly attack rate), run GWAS and selection (LFMM) analysis, and differential gene expression analysis of control/infested cricket body tissues, and plot them for Figure 4 of the text.

## Further data availability

Newly published genome sequencing data are available at the NCBI Sequence Read Archive under accession PRJNA948842. Previously published genome sequencing data from Hawaiian T. oceanicus are at the NCBI SRA under accession PRJNA1019311, and for Australian populations of T. commodus and T. oceanicus are available from the ENA under accession PRJEB39125, previously published RNA-sequencing datasets are available from the NCBI SRA under accession PRJNA344019 (song experiment) and the NCBI Gene Expression Omnibus under accession GSE15139 (infestation experiment). The genome (T. oceanicus v3) used in these analyses is available from the ENA (976985615).
